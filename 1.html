Biography

I am an Associate Research Scientist at Columbia University Medical Center and will soon launch my independent laboratory in the Department of Pediatrics at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. My work explores the molecular and cellular programs that govern hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) in both health and disease, employing mouse genetics, lineage tracing, flow cytometry, single-cell RNA sequencing, and bioinformatics. I have a particular interest in the HSC microenvironment—the niche within and beyond the bone marrow—and how it supports stem cell function throughout life. Through this research, I aim to accelerate HSC-based therapies and advance treatments for immune-related disorders. Motivated students and researchers at all levels—including undergraduates, graduate students, postdocs, and technicians—are encouraged to join the lab.
