Biography

I am a Research Scientist leading an independent laboratory in the Center for Developmental & Regenerative Biology, Cedars-Sinai Guerin Children’s and the Board of Governors Regenerative Medicine Institute at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. Our lab investigates the biology of hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) and their niches under normal and diseased conditions, with a particular focus on how the niche within and beyond the bone marrow shapes HSC behavior. The main research directions we care currently pursuing include: (1) mechanisms of bone marrow HSC maintenance and self-renewal at the intrinsic, niche, and systematic levels; (2) function and regulation of HSCs in the skull and extramedullary tissues (such as the spleen and liver), with special interests in the niche; (3) function and regulation of HSCs and their niches in immune responses, such as the formation of trained immunity and the development of hematological/neurological disorders. We employ a wide range of approaches, including mouse genetics, lineage tracing, flow cytometry, single-cell sequencing, and bioinformatic tools. Our research goal is to generate transformative insights that improve HSC-based therapies for diverse diseases. We committed to fostering a supportive and collaborative lab environment where every member can thrive.
